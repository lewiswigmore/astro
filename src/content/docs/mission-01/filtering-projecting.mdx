Space is vast and full of noise. To find the signal, you must learn to filter your sensor feeds.

### 1. Locking on Target (`where`)

The `where` operator acts as your primary navigation filter. It discards any data that doesn't match your criteria.

<Holodeck initialCode={`AuthenticationEvents
| where result == "Failed Login"
| take 10`} runFuelCost={5} client:load />

### 2. Reconfiguring the HUD (`project` & `extend`)

*   **`project`**: Selects specific columns to display on your HUD.
*   **`extend`**: Calculates new data and adds it to your display without removing existing data.

<Holodeck initialCode={`AuthenticationEvents
| extend event_type = "Login Attempt"
| project timestamp, event_type, username, result
| take 10`} runFuelCost={5} client:load />

### 3. Identifying Unique Signals (`distinct`)

The `distinct` operator removes duplicate records, showing you only the unique values in a column.

<Holodeck initialCode={`// List all unique usernames that have attempted to login
AuthenticationEvents
| distinct username`} runFuelCost={5} client:load />

### 4. Prioritizing Data (`sort` & `top`)

*   **`sort by`**: Reorders your data (default is descending).
*   **`top`**: A shortcut that sorts and takes the first N records in one maneuver.

<Holodeck initialCode={`// Find the most recent login attempts
AuthenticationEvents
| sort by timestamp desc
| take 10`} runFuelCost={5} client:load />
